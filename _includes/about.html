Our Project

A 3 month sailing journey through the Inside Passage to raise awareness of marine debris. Along the way, we will perform beach cleanups along the remote coastlines of BC and Alaska, and collect data as citizen scientists to support conservation research. We are reframing the face of adventure, and inspiring others to be stewards of the earth as they venture into the outdoors.

Our Objectives

  • To be a vessel for citizen science and community partnerships

  • To monitor and collect marine debris and provide insightful and high quality data

  • To connect and liaise with conservationists, researchers and local changemakers

  • To reframe the face of adventure, and inspire others to be stewards of our oceans

  • To embark on our journey with respect for the First Nations People whose lands and waters we will be travelling through

About Us

We are four friends: Mathilde Gordon, Daniel Greenwood, Kirsten Mathison, and Nic Annau, passionate about the protection of our oceans, and connected by our love for the outdoors. We are a team of cavers, climbers, kayakers, circus performers, astronomers, engineers, scientists, educators and campaigners, with various complimentary (and some completely random) skills. We haven’t spent more than a few days at a time together, and hail from different sides of the Pacific Ocean, but we are uniting for a cause close to our hearts.

Potential Partnerships and Projects

  • Conducting scientific research (observational or experimental) for other projects and studies

  • Helping remove the trash we collect from remote beaches (anyone with a skiff)
